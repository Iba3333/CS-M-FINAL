Customer Service & Marketing Quiz

Section 1: Multiple Choice (Understanding)

1. What is Customer Service Expectations Assessment?

📘 Explanation: The text defines it as a process of gathering feedback from customers to evaluate whether a company’s customer service is meeting the expectations of customers.

2. What is the definition of Marketing?

📘 Explanation: The slide "Definition of Marketing" strictly states it is "The management process responsible for identifying, anticipating and satisfying customer requirements profitability."

3. Which of the following is a characteristic of Advertising?

📘 Explanation: Characteristics of Advertising include one way communication, paid activity, and being impersonal in nature.

4. What is the difference between data and information?

📘 Explanation: The slide states: Data is ‘raw’ facts and/or figures, generally numerical. Information is data that has been organized, processed and is presented with context.

5. Which target market strategy focuses efforts on targeting a very specific and well-defined segment of the market?

📘 Explanation: Concentrated Marketing (Niche marketing) is when the company focuses its efforts on targeting a very specific and well-defined segment of the market.

6. Which of the following is an advantage of Primary Research?

📘 Explanation: Primary Research advantages include being up-to-date, relevant, accurate, reliable, confidential, and cost-effective.

7. In Porter’s Five Forces, what evaluates the influence that suppliers have on the industry?

📘 Explanation: The "Power of Suppliers" force evaluates the influence that suppliers have on the industry and addresses how easily they can drive up the cost of inputs.

8. What is the aim of a SWOT Analysis?

📘 Explanation: The aim of a SWOT analysis is to convert weaknesses into strengths and threats into opportunities.

9. Which pricing strategy is used when a business charges a high initial price, and then lowers it over time?

📘 Explanation: Price Skimming is when businesses charge a high initial price and lower it over time, often for new/innovative luxury products.

10. Which extension of PESTLE analysis adds a consideration of ethical factors?

📘 Explanation: STEEPLE Analysis adds Social, Technological, Economic, Environmental, Political, Legal, and Ethical factors.

Section 2: True or False (Understanding)

11. Customer Service Expectations Assessment can help identify areas of improvement and provide a way of measuring customer satisfaction.

📘 Explanation: The overview explicitly states it can help identify areas of improvement, as well as provide a way of measuring customer satisfaction.

12. Publicity uses first and second person voice in an effort to sell you something.

📘 Explanation: False. Advertising uses first and second person voice to sell. Publicity uses objective and unbiased third person voice.

13. In business markets compared to consumer markets, purchases may occur much less frequently.

📘 Explanation: True. Business markets are characterized by higher value purchases that occur much less frequently than consumer markets.

14. To mitigate the effects of changes in logistics services, a company may implement a phased approach or offer compensation in the form of reduced rates.

📘 Explanation: True. The text lists both of these as methods to mitigate the effects of changes on customers.

15. Differentiated marketing is a strategy where a company promotes a single product or service to a broad and general audience.

📘 Explanation: False. That defines Mass Marketing. Differentiated Marketing targets multiple market segments with unique approaches.

16. Secondary research is information provided by others and is usually the cheapest and quickest option.

📘 Explanation: True. It is also known as desk research and is usually the cheapest and quickest option.

17. One advantage of observations is that it measures the reasons for certain behavior and can uncover motivation or attitudes.

📘 Explanation: False. This is listed as a disadvantage: Observations do NOT measure the reasons for certain behavior and cannot uncover motivation.

18. When close substitutes are available, customers have the option to switch, and the company's power may be weakened.

📘 Explanation: True. According to the Threat of Substitutes slide, having close substitutes weakens a company's power.

19. Strengths in a SWOT analysis refer to the external capabilities and resources of an organization.

📘 Explanation: False. Strengths are the INTERNAL capabilities and resources of an organization.

20. Co-operative pricing is when the business prices its products lower than competitors while maintaining healthy margins.

📘 Explanation: False. Co-operative pricing is matching competitors' prices. Pricing lower is called Aggressive Pricing.

📘 Model Answer: 1. Need Recognition, 2. Information Search, 3. Evaluation of Alternatives, 4. Purchase Decision, 5. Post Purchase Evaluation.

24. List the 3 strategic options for market targeting.

📘 Model Answer: 1. Mass Marketing, 2. Differentiated Marketing, 3. Concentrated Marketing.

25. List the 4 basic activities to meet a target market’s expectations regarding customer support and engagement.

📘 Model Answer: 1. Availability and Accessibility, 2. Responsive Service, 3. Encourage Customer Feedback, 4. Loyalty Programs and Incentives.

26. What does PESTLE stand for?

📘 Model Answer: Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental, and Legal.

27. (Exam) Provide three examples of internal Weaknesses in a SWOT analysis.

📘 Model Answer: (Any 3 of the following): Lack of skilled labor, High workforce turnover, Lack of investment, Poor communications, Lack of management commitment issues, Issues with suppliers, Lack of resources.

28. (Expl) Explain the "Threat of New Entrants" in Porter’s Five Forces.

📘 Model Answer: This force examines the possibility of new companies entering the industry. It examines how easy or difficult it is for new competitors to enter the market and how much they could disrupt existing businesses. The quicker and cheaper it is to enter, the more weakened an established company's position may be.

29. List 3 advantages of Questionnaires.

📘 Model Answer: (Any 3): Flexibility in delivery, Can cover a large number of people/organizations, Wide geographic coverage, Relatively cheap, No prior arrangements are needed, Respondent has time to think, No interviewer bias.

30. List 3 disadvantages of Focus Groups.

📘 Model Answer: (Any 3): They can be hard to control, Responses of each participant are not independent, A few members can dominate the group, The data require skill and experience to analyze.

31. List the 10 Benefits of Customer Service Expectations Assessment.

32. Compare the steps of the Consumer Decision Making Process with the Organizational Buying Process.

📘 Model Answer: Consumer: 1. Need recognition, 2. Information search, 3. Evaluation of alternatives, 4. Purchase decision, 5. Post purchase evaluation.
Organizational: 1. Problem, 2. Product Specification, 3. Supplier Search, 4. Supplier Selection, 5. Order Placed, 6. Performance evaluation.

33. (Expl) How might changes in logistics services affect existing customers, and what 5 actions can be taken to mitigate these effects?

📘 Model Answer: Not all customers will be affected equally; some may benefit and some may be disadvantaged. To mitigate the effects, we may: 1. Implement a phased approach. 2. Only apply them to new customers. 3. Offer compensation in the form of reduced rates. 4. Delay price rises. 5. Offer additional, value-adding services.

34. (Expl) Explain the "Power of Buyers" in Porter's Five Forces and provide an example.

📘 Model Answer: This force examines the power that buyers (customers) have over the industry. It is affected by how many buyers a company has, how important each customer is, and how much it would cost the company to find new customers. Example: Large buyers who purchase in bulk may have greater negotiating power.

35. (Expl) Describe the three variations of Competitive Pricing strategies.

📘 Model Answer: 1. Co-operative pricing: The business matches its competitors’ prices (e.g., petrol stations). 2. Aggressive pricing: The business prices its products lower than competitors while maintaining healthy margins. 3. Dismissive pricing: The business prices its products as it wishes (premium product/service), but this may create vulnerability.
